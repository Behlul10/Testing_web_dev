Commonly used Materials & why

- Brass: Can be designed to be durable and quiet - Iron copper plastic: Can be used for cabinets, toy storage, and other doors - Steel: Can be high grade and used for doors with a weight limit of up to 25 kilograms - Stainless steel: A popular choice because it's rust-resistant and can withstand frequent use

Historical Background

Paul Giffard
Looking at the earliest forms of the pneumatics we can see the French Engineer Paul Giffard.
In 1872 Giffard received his patent for a new gun design that used removable cartridges filled with compressed air, compressed gasses, and liquified gas as the propelling force for the gun.

Lifespan

Pneumatic hinges aren't useful on their own, they are used for something, like hinges. Meaning they are part of a bigger design, which meet their lifecycle when the bigger design dies. If a car gets totaled, the pneumatic hinge is useless, if a building collapses the door hinge is useless. There is an exception, you can reuse the pneumatic hinge for other designs if the hinge alone isn't damaged.
